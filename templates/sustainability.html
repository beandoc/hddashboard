Dialysate Flow Rate (Qd)
Reducing Qd 500→300 mL/min saves 48 L ultrapure water and 0.14 kWh RO electricity per session (measured on Baxter AK98, same unit model). High-flux membranes retain ≥92% urea clearance at Qd 300. Confirm Kt/V ≥1.4 after 4 weeks.

Dialysate Temperature
Reducing from 37°C → 35.5°C cuts machine heater energy 7–10% and significantly reduces intradialytic hypotension episodes. Endorsed by KDIGO and ISN guidelines. Each 1°C reduction saves ~3.5% of dialysis machine electricity (2.2 kWh/session baseline).

Dialyser Reuse Programme
Reprocess high-flux dialysers with 3.5% peracetic acid — safe for HBV/HCV/HIV-negative patients; CPCB BMWM Rules 2016 compliant. Monitor TCV; discard if <80% or any visible clotting. LCI factor: 1.35 kg CO₂e/dialyser (Polyflux 210H — polycarbonate + polysulfone housing).

Disposable Gowns & Drapes → Washable Cotton
CSSD-laundered cotton gowns and draw-sheets produce ~10% of the lifetime carbon of equivalent single-use disposables. Retain single-use only for infectious-precaution sessions (HBsAg+, known sepsis). Standard practice in government nephrology units across Maharashtra.

HDF → Standard High-Flux HD Conversion
For HDF patients where the original indication has resolved or marginal middle-molecule benefit no longer justifies the extra resources — conversion to high-flux HD saves the HDF set plus the substitution fluid water per session. Clinical benefit of HDF over HFHD is marginal in well-dialysed patients (ESHOL trial, Turkish Online HDF trial).

Red-Bag Waste Segregation Improvement
Dry outer packaging (saline bag wrappers, tubing boxes, paper) is routinely misclassified as red-bag CBWTF waste across Indian HD units. Correct segregation moves this to dry recycling — reducing autoclave weight, treatment cost, and carbon. Typically halves red-bag weight with no change to clinical practice.

Qb-Matched Dialysate Flow Protocol
Instead of a universal 500 mL/min, set Qd per patient based on their blood flow: Qd = 1.2 × Qb (clamped 300–800 mL/min). Peer-reviewed evidence shows this ratio maintains equivalent Kt/V to fixed 500 mL/min (1.29±0.16 vs. 1.26±0.21, p = 0.068) with a potential 20–25% dialysate reduction. Applicable to any HD machine — nursing staff set Qd each session from the Qb displayed on the machine.
⚠ Use as an alternative to Lever 1 (fixed Qd reduction) — activating both simultaneously double-counts the same water saving.

Citrate vs. Heparin Anticoagulation
Heparin is extracted from porcine intestinal mucosa — a carbon-intensive animal-derived process. Switching to citrate (derived from beet molasses fermentation) achieves ~99% reduction in anticoagulant CO₂e per session. Citrate is also associated with reduced catheter-related bloodstream infections. van der Meulen KIR 2024 · Fan & Favaloro Lancet Haematology 2022 · Backston et al. Hemodialysis Int 2025.

Central Acid Delivery System
Replacing individual 5 L acid concentrate containers with a centralised system (1,000 L bulk containers + 3,000 L storage tanks) eliminates high-frequency truck deliveries. A 30-bed HD unit saves up to 33,841 kg CO₂e/year on logistics emissions alone. An alternative: dry/semi-dry powder concentrate reduces transport carbon fourfold. Murcutt et al. J Nephrology 2024 · Zawierucha et al. KBP Res 2023.

Home Hemodialysis Conversion
Transitioning suitable in-centre patients to home HD eliminates round-trip patient travel — one of the largest transport footprint components. Travel savings: 660–870 kg CO₂e/patient/year (765 kg midpoint). Home HD additionally uses lower dialysate flow rates and generates less packaging waste per session. In India, ~60% of patients travel >50 km to access dialysis, making home HD a high-impact equity and climate co-intervention. Connor et al. Hemodial Int 2011 · James J Renal Care 2007 · Backston et al. Hemodialysis Int 2025.

💧 Water
  • Up to 70% of treated water discarded with older RO systems; 20–60% with modern ones
  • Rejected water meets WHO potable water criteria — suitable for sanitation, irrigation, and fish aquaculture reuse
  • Reducing Qd 500→400 mL/min saves ≥48 L/session = 194,688 L/year for 26 patients
  • Qd 300 mL/min maintains comparable adequacy (Kt/V, URR, potassium) — Rydzewska-Rosołowska CKJ 2024
  • Personalized HD (real-time optical toxin monitoring): −131 L water and −1.22 kWh/session → 102.2 million m³ water saved globally by 2030
  • NephroCare France: 50% reduction in water/treatment after HD machine upgrade
  • HDF optimized at Qd/Qb 1.2 + Qb >350 mL/min achieves Kt/V 1.70 at 430 mL/min Qd (26,000-patient real-world cohort)
⚡ Energy
  • Global healthcare = 4.4% of all GHG (equivalent to 514 coal-fired power plants)
  • One HD facility/year ≈ 93 homes (769,374 kg CO₂e); single session ≈ driving 238 km (58.9 kg CO₂e)
  • Solar power: 76.5% cost reduction in HD electricity; ROI in as little as 3 years (updated estimates)
  • Micro hydro-turbine on RO reject water: 300 kg CO₂e/year reduction per unit
  • Central acid delivery: 33,841 kg CO₂e/year saved at 30-bed unit vs individual 5 L containers
  • Dry/semi-dry powder concentrate: 4× transport carbon reduction
  • Home HD travel savings: 660–870 kg CO₂e/patient/year
  • Citrate vs heparin anticoagulation: ~99% CO₂e reduction for anticoagulant component
🗑️ Waste
  • 5–8 kg plastic waste/HD session; ~half (2.5–2.9 kg) is uncontaminated and recyclable
  • HD units globally produce an estimated 750 million tons of plastic waste
  • PVC mixed with other plastics renders the entire batch non-recyclable; incineration releases toxic dioxins
  • 100,000 HD machines discarded globally/year ≈ 12,500 tons — dismantling requires ≥35 h/machine
  • APD waste pilot: 59% PVC recovery rate; break-even at 263 patients/month, avoiding 5 kg CO₂, 13 L water, 20 kWh per kg virgin PVC replaced
🌱 Prevention & Future
  • Kidney transplantation: 95.7% environmental impact reduction vs HD; 90.9% vs PD
  • Incremental dialysis: improved QoL, preserved residual kidney function, no mortality difference
  • Plant-based diet: the most impactful single lifestyle modification for CKD prevention and environmental footprint
  • Sorbent dialysis: <5 L water per session, suitable for home and portable/emergency use
  • India-specific: ~60% patients travel >50 km; limited infrastructure and OOP costs remain barriers — green nephrology must be inclusive and equitable
  • GREEN-K initiative (ISN) + European Green Deal + KDIGO Controversies Conference on Green Dialysis 2025 driving global policy

This calculator implements levers directly supported by this review. Additional interventions (solar integration, sorbent systems, machine upgrades) are described in the paper but require capital investment outside this simulation scope.
